# Reporting Standards

The current unified style remains unchanged. Two alternative versions now sit
beside it so they can be compared without replacing the original.

## Main versions

| Version | Character | Best for | Graph explanation | Density |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Current unified](report-design.md) | Precise, dark, audit-oriented | Canonical research reports and mixed audiences | Standard titles, subtitles, reads, and provenance | Medium to high |
| [Mellow](report-design-mellow.md) | Calm, soft, minimal | Notes, short decisions, negative results, and reports with few visuals | One concise reading and limitation per graph | Low |
| [Graph-guided](report-design-graph-guided.md) | Explanatory, evidence-first | Complex quant reports and readers who need help interpreting graphs | Required how-to-read, observation, relevance, and limitation blocks | Medium to high |

## Quick selection

- Choose **current unified** when you want the established house style.
- Choose **mellow** when the material should feel quiet and simple.
- Choose **graph-guided** when misunderstanding a chart is the main reporting
  risk.

All three versions preserve the same research obligations: visible uncertainty,
sample support, information boundaries, costs, limitations, provenance, and
accessibility. A simpler visual profile does not permit weaker evidence.

## Preserved source and companion documents

- `report-design-liq.md` is the preserved general editorial source system.
- `report-design-ml.md` is the preserved quantitative evidence source system.
- `report-design-dlmm.md` is a domain companion for DLMM, execution, and
  strategy-specific reports.

The unified standard resolves conflicts between the source systems through an
explicit report profile. Research validity, uncertainty, and promotion status
come from the active framework; visual polish cannot upgrade weak evidence.
